Grammar Focus: Comparison of Adjectives & Adverbs

Use -er / -est

For one-syllable adjectives and adverbs and two-syllable adjectives ending in -y.

  • quick → quicker → the quickest
  • easy → easier → the easiest
  • hard → harder → the hardest

Use more / most

For longer adjectives and adverbs ending in -ly.

  • useful → more useful → most useful
  • quickly → more quickly → most quickly

Exceptions

Some words have irregular forms.

  • good/well → better → best
  • bad/badly → worse → worst
  • far → further → furthest

Grammar Focus: Present Perfect & Past Simple

Past Simple

Used for things which started and finished in the past.

"I joined my company three years ago."

Present Perfect

Used for past events with a connection to the present.

  • Still happening: "I 've been given a lot more responsibility."
  • Time is not stated: "I 've been to New York three times."
  • A present result: "I 've lost my job (and now I'm unemployed)."
